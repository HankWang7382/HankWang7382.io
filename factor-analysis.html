Factor effectiveness is evaluated by applying factor construction methodologies referenced from global asset managers, index providers, and academic literature to the Taiwan equity universe. Each factor is assessed across three dimensions: (1) historical long-short performance and Sharpe significance, (2) return persistence and stability over time, and (3) the cross-sectional relationship between factor exposure and future individual stock returns. Factors meeting the effectiveness criteria are then combined into a composite style factor.

* Score = average length (in months) of consecutive positive-return streaks within each rolling 10-year window, then averaged across all windows. A score of 1 indicates no meaningful persistence — positive returns are not followed by another positive month. A score of 2 means positive returns tend to persist for 2 consecutive months on average. Scores above 3 suggest the factor generates sustained positive returns, making it more reliable for systematic strategies.

Factor-Return Relationship

Fama-MacBeth cross-sectional regression at each rebalancing · Model: Individual Stock Future Return = β₀ + β₁·Beta + β₂·Factor Value · β₂ = factor return · T-test on mean β₂ = 0

Factor 1M Coef1M p-val 3M Coef3M p-val 6M Coef6M p-val 12M Coef12M p-val

* At each rebalancing date, a cross-sectional regression is run on individual stocks: Annualized Individual Stock Future Return = β₀ + β₁·Beta(Z-score) + β₂·Factor Value(Z-score). β₂ estimates the factor return — the marginal contribution of factor exposure to future returns, controlling for market beta. Future returns are annualized (1M ×12, 3M ×4, 6M ×2, 12M ×1). A two-sided t-test (H₀: mean β₂ = 0) is then applied. Cell shading: dark green = p < 0.001, medium green = p < 0.01, light green = p < 0.05, grey = not significant.
